## Description [4000]
```
Wake is an elegant menu bar companion that tracks your Mac's battery health,
power draw, and charging habits in real time — no admin password, no kernel
extensions, just standard macOS APIs.

LIVE POWER IN YOUR MENU BAR
See real-time wattage at a glance. Wake combines total system power draw while
plugged in with the battery discharge rate on battery, so the number always
reflects what your Mac is actually using.

DYNAMIC ISLAND IN THE NOTCH
A panel that hugs the notch, blends with the hardware when collapsed, and
expands on hover or click with a bouncy spring and haptic feedback — showing
power, battery health, and temperature, with quick toggles.

BATTERY HEALTH & DECAY LOG
Track maximum capacity, cycle count, and health percentage. Wake keeps an
automatic timeline that records the date and cycle count every time your maximum
capacity changes, so you can watch degradation over the life of your Mac.

SESSION TRACKING
Screen-on time and sleep duration per battery interval, with restart and
shutdown detection and an efficiency readout of screen time per 1% drop.

SMART ADAPTER ANALYSIS
Nominal wattage, genuine-Apple verification, the port in use (MagSafe, USB-C, or
Thunderbolt), a slow-charging alert, and a history of every charger you've used.

TRANSLUCENT DESKTOP WIDGET
A floating, lockable widget with an Apple-style circular battery indicator,
battery temperature, and cycle count — place it anywhere on your desktop.

SMART ALERTS
Fast-drain detection, a high-temperature warning when the battery exceeds 38°C,
and a long-term reminder if you leave the Mac plugged in at full charge for a day.

Wake launches at login (optional), adapts to light and dark mode, and lives
quietly in the menu bar with no Dock icon.
```

## App Review — Notes (CRITICAL — paste verbatim)
```
Wake is a menu-bar-only utility (LSUIElement). After launch it has NO Dock icon
and NO main window — it appears as a battery icon in the macOS menu bar at the
top-right of the screen. Click that icon to open the main panel (Session,
History, Hardware, Settings). A Dynamic Island panel also appears near the notch
on notch-equipped Macs.

The app uses only public, non-privileged APIs (IOKit IORegistry battery
properties and IOPowerSources) and requires no special permissions. It optionally
requests local-notification permission for battery alerts. No account, no login,
no network, no data collection.
```
